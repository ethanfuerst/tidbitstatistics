I'm in the minority of my friends in one regard: I rate movies on IMDB. It's a great way to keep track of movies and TV shows. Here's how my ratings break down:

[![How I breakdown my ratings](/images/3-13-20-table1.png "How I breakdown my ratings")]({% link /html/3-12-20-table1.html %})

I don't rate every movie I see though. For example, I have no clue how to rate most R-rated comedies. I can't figure out how The Hangover compares to any of the movies in my ratings. I also don't really rate series, like Star-Wars or any Marvel film for the same reason.

Let's talk about IMDb's rating system. Currently, the highest rated movie on IMDb is [The Shawshank Redemption](https://www.imdb.com/chart/top/?ref_=nv_mv_250), with a 9.2/10. I wish that I could rate movies with a decimal rating and not just a whole number partially because the average ratings are rounded to one decimal place. Anyway, I'm interested to see the relationship between my rating and IMDb's rating.

[![IMDb Rating vs. My Rating](/images/3-13-20-scatter1.png "IMDb Rating vs. My Rating")]({% link /html/3-12-20-scatter1.html %})

When I compare an IMDb rating with my rating directly, I get to see which movies I liked more than IMDb and less than IMDb.

[![IMDb Rating vs. My Rating breakdown](/images/3-13-20-table2.png "IMDb Rating vs. My Rating breakdown")]({% link /html/3-12-20-table2.html %})

A positive difference in ratings and a green cell color means that I rated the movie higher than IMDb did, and if it's negative with a blue cell color then IMDb liked it more than I did. The cells with a yellow color are movies that I rated the same as IMDb.

I wonder if there is a relationship betwee the year a movie is realeased and the difference between ratings. I suspect that I rate movies released before 2000 lower than IMDb on average.

[![Year vs. Difference in Ratings](/images/3-13-20-scatter2.png "Year vs. Difference in Ratings")]({% link /html/3-12-20-scatter2.html %})

__So what actually is my taste in movies?__

Whenever someone asks me what my taste in movies is, I usually say that my favorite kind of movie is a 2010's Sci-Fi movie. I also tell them I'm not a huge fan of older movies. But is this actually the case? Do I really like new movies more and do I really like sci-fi movies the most?

There's two parts to this question: what decade of movies do I like the most and what genre do I like the most. 

First, I'm gonna see when most of the movies I have rated were released.

__What is the distribution of the release year of my ratings?__

[![Number of movies in my ratings by year released](/images/3-13-20-bar1.png "Number of movies in my ratings by year released")]({% link /html/3-12-20-bar1.html %})

So most of the movies that I have seen were made after around 2010, and I haven't seen multiple movies from years before 2000. Except for 1994, from which I have seen Forrest Gump, Pulp Fiction, and Speed.

__Do I really like older movies better?__

To look at this, I binned the 'Year' column in my dataset to create a new column called 'Decade'. Now I wanted to look at the distribution of my ratings over each decade.

[![My ratings by decade](/images/3-13-20-boxandwhisker1.png "My ratings by decade")]({% link /html/3-12-20-boxandwhisker1.html %})

Most of the movies I have rated were released in the 2000's and 2010's, where there is a large spread of my ratings. The 90's didn't have any 9's or 10's, but also had nothing lower than a 7. The 80's only had movies that I rated a 6, 7 or 8. So far, the 2020's have been rough as my highest rating was a 6. So do I like newer movies better? Kind of, but it's also becuase most of the movies I have seen are from 2000 or later. I'm sure if I watched more movies from before 2000 I would find some I liked, I just have a small sample size.

__How do the genres break up by decade?__

This is kinda tricky because IMDb allows for one movie to have multiple genres. 

To solve this, I used something called one-hot encoding. One-hot encoding is the process of creating new columns that contain either tru or false if that row has the attribute. For example, I would create a new column for each genre in my ratings and change the value to 'True' if the movie on the row was that genre.

There were 20 diffrent genres though, so I'm only going to focus on my top 8 genres right now.

[![My ratings by genre](/images/3-13-20-boxandwhisker2.png "My ratings by genre")]({% link /html/3-12-20-boxandwhisker2.html %})

Action movies and adventure movies were the genres that I rated the highest. My lowest rating was actually a sci-fi movie. For almost all the genres, 8 is my median rating. Adventure was the only genre without a perfect 10 rating from me.

This was a cool exercise to explore my IMDb ratings, but it's not finished yet. I'd like to drill down more in to the year movies were released vs the genre. I'd also like to look at comparing each cluster of decade and genre to what IMDb's average rating was. Eventually, I'd like to create a machine learning model that can predict my rating given the year, genre(s) and IMDb rating of a film.

In order to do that though, I'm gonna need some more data. That means watching more movies!
