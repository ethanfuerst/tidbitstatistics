It was just over a month ago when [Adam Silver suspended the NBA season](https://twitter.com/wojespn/status/1237914166285008896) after Rudy Gobert tested positive for COVID-19, but it feels like a year ago. I was watching Mavs-Nuggets at the time. You could tell when Cuban found out the news. [He freaked out.](https://twitter.com/espn/status/1237919525590441985) About a week went by of various businesses closing down and restrictions being placed.

Ever since then, there's been an NBA-sized hole in our lives. I'm hoping to fill part of that hole right now. As I self-quarantine, I've been hard at work pulling NBA shot data and making shot charts of some of the most memorable performances and stretches of games in recent memory. So without further explanation: here we go!

Side note: The Mavs won the last NBA game of the season. If the season is cancelled, are they champs?

## Linsanity

Remember [Linsanity](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OJa7G-AGgOE)?

![Linsanity](/images/4-17-20-lin.png)

For about a month and a half in 2012, Jeremy Lin was putting on a show. His layups were falling, he was shooting well from 3, and he was getting to the line. This stretch would be cut short by injury but it would live on in the NBA forever. Jeremy Lin got a ring last season with the Toronto Raptors.
